🌐 Connect Your Domain to Your Website

A simple, step-by-step guide — no technical experience needed

What this guide does: Your website is already set up and ready. This guide walks you through one simple task — pointing your GoDaddy domain name to your new website, so when anyone types your address into a browser, they land on your site. The whole process takes about 10 minutes, then up to 24 hours for the internet to fully update.
🌐 Part 1 of 2
Update Your DNS at GoDaddy
1

Log in to GoDaddy

Go to godaddy.com and sign in with the account you used to buy your domain.

2

Find your domain's DNS settings

  • Click your name or account icon in the top right
  • Click My Products
  • Find your domain name and click DNS or Manage DNS next to it
3

Add 4 "A Records" — these point your main domain to GitHub

You need to add four records, one at a time. Click Add (or Add Record) and fill in exactly as shown below. Repeat four times — once for each row:

Add these 4 records (same Type and Name each time, different Value):
Type Name Value TTL
A@185.199.108.1531 Hour
A@185.199.109.1531 Hour
A@185.199.110.1531 Hour
A@185.199.111.1531 Hour
What is "@"? The @ symbol means "the root domain itself" — so yourcharity.org (without www).
4

Add a "CNAME Record" — this handles the www version

Click Add again and fill in:

Type
CNAME
Name
www
Value
your-org.github.io
TTL
1 Hour

Replace your-org with your actual GitHub username (your website coordinator can confirm this).

⚠️ Important: GoDaddy may already have a CNAME record for "www" pointing somewhere else. If so, edit that existing record instead of adding a new one — you cannot have two CNAME records for "www".
5

Save everything

Make sure you click Save after each record. When done, your DNS records list should show the 4 A records and 1 CNAME you just added.

✅ Part 2 of 2
Wait, Then Verify It Worked
6

Wait for DNS to update

DNS changes can take anywhere from 15 minutes to 24 hours to spread across the internet. Most of the time it's under an hour. You don't need to do anything — just wait.

Tip: Try opening your domain in a private/incognito browser window — it's less likely to show a cached (old) version.
7

Go back to GitHub Pages and enable HTTPS

Once your site loads, go back to GitHub → Settings → Pages. You should now see a green checkmark and the option to check "Enforce HTTPS". Check that box — it gives your site the secure padlock 🔒 in the browser bar. This is free and automatic.

Don't see the HTTPS option yet? Wait another hour and check again. It takes a bit longer to issue the security certificate.
8

Test your site

Try opening all three of these in your browser — they should all show your website:

  • yourcharity.org
  • www.yourcharity.org
  • Your old GitHub URL: yourorg.github.io/yoursite
